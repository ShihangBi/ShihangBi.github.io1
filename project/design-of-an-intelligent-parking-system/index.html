Design of an Intelligent Parking System

Jan 1, 0001

In the current challenge of escalating urban traffic congestion, the management and utilization of underground parking facilities have become pivotal components of urban traffic management. GPS positioning is often hindered by signal limitations underground, making it difficult to provide accurate location information. Additionally, other positioning technologies face significant challenges in maintaining precise positioning accuracy. In this context, the advantages of WiFi positioning technology are increasingly prominent, making it the ideal choice for intelligent parking systems.

I led a team of five members to complete a project that involved a vehicle detection device and a positioning system based on the WKNN algorithm implemented in Python. The project utilized an ESP8266MINID1 chip and ultrasonic modules to build the vehicle detection device, and data transmission was facilitated through four AP transmission sources. Eventually, a mobile application was developed, allowing users to access real-time location and available parking space information within the underground parking lot. My responsibilities included establishing a cloud platform, programming the ESP8266miniD1 module, developing the parking lot model, and recording experimental videos.
