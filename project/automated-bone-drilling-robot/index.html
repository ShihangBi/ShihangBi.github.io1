Automated Bone Drilling Robot

Jan 1, 0001

Against the backdrop of rapid advancement in the medical machinery sector, we responded to prevalent challenges faced by traditional orthopedic surgeons utilizing handheld bone drills. These issues encompassed deviations in drilling positions, inaccuracies in angles, and notable geometric discrepancies. In response, we engineered an efficient and highly precise orthopedic drilling surgical robot. The overarching objective was to harness automated mechanical systems as substitutes for surgeons, thereby liberating human resources, augmenting drilling precision and efficiency, and mitigating the incidence of medical mishaps.

Conducting thorough research, I delved into the intricacies of a specific orthopedic drilling surgical robot and embarked on an innovative redesign of its mechanical arm structure. By opting for a mobile joint at its extremity, the platform hosted a drilling apparatus, thereby ensuring drilling linearity and diminishing the occurrence of medical mishaps. Deliberate calibration of gear reduction ratios facilitated swift maneuvering towards predetermined operational positions through reverse propulsion. The resultant robot exhibited nuanced control over movement, proficiently rotating the drill bit for drilling while enabling lateral displacement of the drill bit.
